Flora & Fauna of Australia

Eucalyptus

Iconic Australian tree with more than 600 species. Its aromatic leaves are the main food for koalas and its wood is widely used. Many species can regenerate after bushfires.

Acacias (wattle)

Over 900 species adapted to dry soils. The Golden Wattle is the national floral emblem of Australia, known for its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Kangaroo

One of the best-known marsupials. Kangaroos are powerful jumpers, live in groups called “mobs” and symbolize Australia worldwide.

Koala

Tree-dwelling marsupial that feeds almost exclusively on eucalyptus leaves. It sleeps up to 20 hours a day and is a vulnerable species due to habitat loss.

Currency & Visa Information

Work Visa

Working Holiday Visa (subclass 462): For Argentinians aged 18–30

Allows you to work and travel in Australia for 12 months, with the option to extend if requirements are met.

Requirements include: valid passport, studies, proof of funds, health insurance and English knowledge.

Applications are online, with limited spots available each year.

Permanent Residency

Residency Options: Family, work, or business and investment. It lets you live, work, and study in Australia forever, and you can use the health system (Medicare). You can also travel for 5 years, and later it can lead to Australian citizenship if you meet the requirements.

Applications are made through the Department of Home Affairs.

Australian Dollar (AUD)

Symbol: $ or AUD

Coins: 5¢, 10¢, 20¢, 50¢, $1, $2

Banknotes: $5, $10, $20, $50, $100

The Australian Dollar (AUD) is Australia’s money. Its notes are made of plastic, which makes them strong and long-lasting. The coins and notes show important symbols and animals from the country.

Culture & Music

Australia mixes ancient traditions, many cultures, and a love for the outdoors.

Australian Traditions

Aboriginal Heritage: The world’s oldest living culture, with stories, art, and a deep connection to the land.

Multicultural Life: People from 200+ countries make Australia diverse and welcoming.

Outdoors: Beaches, barbecues, and sports are part of daily life.

Music Scene

Australia has a vibrant music scene, from international rock and pop stars to unique electronic sounds. Aboriginal culture introduced the Didgeridoo, a wooden wind instrument with a deep buzzing tone, considered one of the oldest instruments in the world. Today, traditional Aboriginal music blends with contemporary styles, showing the country’s cultural diversity.

Major Festivals & Holidays

Australia Day (January 26): Celebrates the nation’s history, culture and diversity, although it also sparks debate about Indigenous perspectives.

ANZAC Day (April 25): A solemn day of remembrance for Australian and New Zealand soldiers who served and died in wars and conflicts.

Australian Sports

A nation passionate about sport

Cricket

Australia's summer sport, with legendary players like Don Bradman and Steve Smith. The Ashes series against England is a national obsession.

Rugby

Both Rugby League and Rugby Union are hugely popular, with the Wallabies representing Australia internationally.

Australian Rules Football

AFL is the most popular sport in southern Australia, with the Grand Final being one of the biggest sporting events.

Swimming

Australia is a swimming powerhouse, consistently ranking among the top nations at the Olympics with stars like Ian Thorpe and Emma McKeon.

Tennis

Home to the Australian Open, one of tennis's Grand Slams, and legendary players like Rod Laver and Pat Rafter.

Olympic Excellence

Australia consistently punches above its weight at the Olympics, particularly in swimming, cycling, and rowing.
